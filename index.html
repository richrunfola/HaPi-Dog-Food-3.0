The Science of Real Food

Every ingredient earns its place.

We don’t add ingredients to hit a price point or fill a bag. We add them because the research says they matter for your dog’s long-term health.

🥩

Multi-Protein Rotation

Rotating beef, chicken, turkey & pork provides a broader essential amino acid profile than single-protein diets, reducing allergy sensitization risk over time.

🫀

Organ Meats

Liver contains 10–100x the nutrient density of muscle meat. Rich in retinol, B12, folate, iron, and CoQ10 — irreplaceable in a whole-food canine diet.

🥦

Cruciferous Greens

Broccoli and leafy greens deliver sulforaphane, a compound with well-documented anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory properties in mammalian studies.

🎃

Butternut Squash

A prebiotic-rich carbohydrate that feeds beneficial gut bacteria without the blood-sugar spike caused by rice, potato, or pea-based fillers.

🥚

Whole Eggs

One of the most bioavailable protein sources on earth. Eggs score 100 on the biological value scale — matching the protein absorption efficiency of meat itself.

🫐

Blueberries

Anthocyanins in blueberries have been studied for their role in reducing cognitive decline, oxidative stress, and inflammation in aging mammals including dogs.

🐔

Chicken Feet (Glucosamine)

Among the highest food-source concentrations of natural glucosamine — the compound most commonly supplemented for canine joint health, here in whole-food form. Hannah’s joint recovery was built on these.

🚫

What We Leave Out

No rice. No potatoes. No peas. No corn. No “meat by-products.” No artificial preservatives. If it doesn’t belong in your dog’s body, it doesn’t leave our kitchen.
